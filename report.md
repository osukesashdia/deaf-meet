# Methodology

This study adopted a design-oriented methodology to develop a real-time captioning interface that supports Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing users during spoken online interaction. The proposed approach combined speech transcription, audio-prosodic analysis, emotion inference, and visual presentation in a single multimodal pipeline. Speech was captured from the local microphone and converted into text in real time, while the audio stream was analyzed in parallel to extract short-term vocal dynamics.

For the linguistic pathway, each finalized utterance was treated as a textual unit and passed through a text-based emotion inference stage. For the audio pathway, recent speech segments were summarized into interpretable prosodic descriptors, including loudness, pitch, pitch variation, voiced ratio, and interruption intensity. These features were used to estimate the level of vocal activation and to distinguish relatively excited, urgent, calm, or subdued speech patterns.

The final emotional label was determined through a rule-based fusion process combining the text-derived and audio-derived results. When both channels were consistent, the shared result was retained as stronger evidence. When the text signal was weak or neutral but the audio signal suggested clear activation or affective direction, the audio cue was allowed to influence the final classification. When audio evidence was limited, the textual result was preserved. This procedure enabled the system to balance semantic meaning with vocal delivery rather than relying on either channel alone.

The fused result was then presented through a transparent overlay that displayed emotion-coded captions together with a continuously updated rhythm visualization above the text area. This visual design was intended to communicate both verbal content and immediate interactional dynamics, including changes in speaking intensity and interruption. During iterative evaluation, intermediate outputs, including transcript, text result, audio result, and fused result, were also monitored in order to examine how effectively vocal tone changes were reflected in the final emotion assignment.
